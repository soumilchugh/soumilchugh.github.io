Hi, I'm Soumil Chugh

Engineer & Builder

Research Engineer @ Huawei, Canada

I am currently working as a Research Engineer at the Human Machine Interaction (HMI) Lab with Huawei, Toronto, Canada. My work involves developing user-centric technologies, including advanced eye-tracking systems and intelligent agents for enhancing interaction in system-level applications.

Previously, I earned my MASc in Computer Engineering from the University of Toronto, under the guidance of Prof. Jonathan Rose and Prof. Moshe Eizenman, focusing on VR eye-tracking systems.

Prior Experience

I completed my undergraduate studies in Electronics and Communication at Panjab University, Chandigarh, India. Post-graduation, I worked at Jana Care in India, where I was part of the R&D team that developed FDA-approved portable medical devices and a robotic system for HbA1c tests.

Later, I contributed to building General Prognostics Inc., a remote disease management system.

Career Mission & Contributions

My mission is to develop systems that directly impact end-users by advancing human-machine interactions. This includes projects like eye-tracking systems at Huawei and intelligent agent systems for operating system-level applications.
