CURRENT RESEARCH

My main area of research is to design cross-platform camera-based eye tracking systems. The platforms include Virtual Reality headsets, smartphones, laptops/desktops and in-car systems. While devices such as smartphones and laptops/desktops have in-built RGB webcams that are used for monitoring a person's eye movements, for devices such as VR headsets and in-car systems, an Infrared based optical system is used. Over the last 4 years, I have filed 3 patents and written 2 papers mainly focussing on algorithm development. The algorithms include traditional computer vision techniques, deep learning-based methods and a hybrid approach. My current research focus is on driver monitoring systems. The issue with the state-of-the-art eye tracking systems inside a car is their sensitivity towards head movement. We are currently working towards an algorithm that is robust to head movements while also exhibiting the accuracy required by eye tracking inside a car.
