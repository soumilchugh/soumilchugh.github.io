Hi, I'm Soumil Chugh - Engineer/Builder

Research Engineer @ HMI Lab, Huawei, Toronto, Canada.

About

I am a Research Engineer in the Human Machine Interaction (HMI) Lab at Huawei, Toronto, Canada. My journey in the field began with the completion of my Masters of Applied Science (MASc) in Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, Canada. Under the supervision of Prof. Jonathan Rose and Prof. Moshe Eizenman from the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department, my thesis focused on designing an Eye Tracking System for Virtual Reality (VR) Headsets.

Prior Experience

I graduated from Panjab University, India in 2015, with a major in Electronics and Communication (ECE). Before pursuing my masters at the University of Toronto, I worked as a software and hardware engineer at Jana Care, India, where I was part of the R&D team that designed and developed FDA approved smartphone-enabled portable medical devices.

Current Research

My main area of research is designing cross-platform camera-based eye tracking systems, including platforms such as Virtual Reality headsets, smartphones, laptops/desktops, and in-car systems. Over the past four years, I have filed three patents and written two papers, mainly focusing on algorithm development. My current research focus is on driver monitoring systems. I am working on an algorithm that is robust to head movements while maintaining the accuracy required for eye tracking inside a car.
